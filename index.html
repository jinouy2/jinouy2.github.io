United States: Weekly Number of Deaths Year 2017-20

Maximize window size for optimal presentation. Click along year tabs to view. Data as of July 22, 2020.

Seasonality presented in a given year (i.e. 2017):

More deaths in winter and less in summer.

Similar seasonality was observed in 2018, which is highlighted in blue. Previous year(s) marked in grey for comparison.

Worse influenza season leaded to higher deaths count in 2018 winter, comparing to same period in 2017.

Seasonality observed in 2019 (highlighted in blue) as expected. Previous year(s) marked in grey for comparison.

2020 experience is highlighed in blue, with solid and dashed line representing deaths count including and excluding COVID-19 respectively. Experience from 2017 to 2019 is marked in grey for comparison.

2020 is an anomaly year due to COVID-19 outbreak. First quarter followed the same pattern as previous years. However, excess deaths were recorded starting week 12, which was the start of the outbreak in United States.

After excluding COVID-19 deaths (dashed line), there's still excess deaths observed since start of pandemic.

The rest of the excess deaths could be COVID-19 or other causes. However, it is hard to tell without massive COVID-19 testing.
